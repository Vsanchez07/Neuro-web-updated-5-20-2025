Movement disorders are neurological conditions that affect the body's ability to move smoothly and purposefully. They can manifest as involuntary or excessive movements, as well as difficulties with coordination and balance. Some common types of movement disorders include Parkinson's disease, dystonia, essential tremor, and Huntington's disease.

Hyperkinetic Movement Disorders: These involve excessive or uncontrolled movements.

  • Tremors: Involuntary, rhythmic shaking, often in the hands or head. Essential tremor is a common type.
  • Dystonia: Involuntary muscle contractions causing twisting movements or abnormal postures.
  • Chorea: Involuntary, rapid, and irregular movements. Huntington's disease is a well-known example.
  • Tic Disorders: Sudden, repetitive, and involuntary movements or sounds. Tourette syndrome is a common example.
  • Restless Legs Syndrome: An irresistible urge to move the legs, often accompanied by uncomfortable sensations.
